Cinzel + Cormorant Garamond

Cinzel headings — Roman imperial capitals. Used by Dark Souls, Elden Ring, countless dark fantasy games. Authoritative and unmistakable.
Cormorant Garamond body — elegant old-world serif, very readable at small sizes. Claude Garamond meets fantasy.

IM Fell English (All)

Based on actual 17th-century typefaces by John Fell. Has rough, inky edges — looks like it was printed on a hand press. Extremely atmospheric. Most "old book" feeling of all options. May sacrifice some readability at very small sizes.

Almendra (All)

Designed specifically for fantasy RPG use. Calligraphic pen-stroke character with slight swoops and flourishes. Very D&D. Works as both heading and body. Most "fantasy game" of the options.

MedievalSharp + Uncial Antiqua + Cormorant

MedievalSharp for major headings — angular, calligraphic, unmistakably medieval.
Uncial Antiqua for labels/nav — Celtic manuscript style.
Cormorant Garamond for body — keeps long text readable. Maximum fantasy, 3-font stack.

My Take

Cinzel + Cormorant Garamond is my recommendation — it's the dark fantasy gold standard (literally used by FromSoftware games), looks incredible at every size, and the pairing is battle-tested. But if you want maximum old-world atmosphere, IM Fell English is unmatched. The medieval options are fun but risk tipping into "costume" territory for a tool you use regularly.
