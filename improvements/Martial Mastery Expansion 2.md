# Armorers' Revision

**Armorer’s Revision**  
Armor Class (AC) is based on the assemblage of armor that a creature is wearing. One’s AC is determined in reference to the steps and chart below.

**Determining AC** involves three considerations: the base AC, the Dexterity bonus, and the armor pieces.

1. Base AC is determined by whether the armor worn is suitable for dodging hits, weathering blows, or something in between.  
   1. Light armor has a base AC 6  
   2. Medium armor has a base AC of 8  
   3. Heavy armor has a base AC of 0  
2. Each armor weight class is associated with a Dexterity bonus. For the purposes of the DEX bonus, a creature is considered as heavily armored as the heaviest piece of armor they currently wear.  
   1. Lightly armored creatures add their DEX bonus to their base AC  
   2. Medium armored creatures add their DEX bonus to a maximum of 2  
   3. Heavily armored creatures do not add their dexterity bonus to their AC  
3. Each individual armor piece adds a bonus to the creature’s total AC, as listed in the table below.

**Some considerations for selecting armor types** include the fact that Heavy armor pieces are too heavy for any creatures with a strength score **lower than 13** to benefit from, and any **creatures wearing heavy armor have disadvantage on stealth checks**.

Additionally, **any individual piece of armor can be Runed**, but a creature can only attune to one armor rune at a time.

***“…His Armor Rent…”***  
When a target is hit with a [Critical Hit](), one piece of armor they are wearing will become *Rent*, and lose its effectiveness. Rended pieces of armor can be fixed one at a time during a short rest (or a number of pieces equal to one’s proficiency bonus during a long rest). When hit with a [lingering injury](), one piece of armor will instead be broken (must be reforged) if it is covering the area hit.

# Critical Hits and Lingering Injuries

**Critical Hits**  
When a creature wears armor, it raises their AC and defends them from taking damage. But sometimes, a warrior is hit *just so*, and the armor itself is damaged. This typically occurs when that warrior suffers a **Critical Hit**.

**Critical Hits** usually occur when a creature makes an attack roll, and the number on their dice is within their “crit range” before adding any attack roll modifiers. The crit range is usually only 20, and so ***natural 20s*** **on attack rolls lead to critical hits**. When a creature rolls a critical hit, they will **double the values rolled on all damage dice for the initial impact of that attack.**

After rolling a critical hit, a creature has a chance to deliver a **Lingering Injury** to their target, some more serious harm that impairs the creature. To see if this is the case, they must roll a **“Crit Confirm”**, which is a repeat of their attack role (without advantage or disadvantage) against the target's AC as it was when they first made their attack. Upon a **success**, proceed to the lingering injuries section below, before dealing the critical damage. On a Natural 20, the attacker may choose a lingering injury to inflict on their target.

Upon a **failure**, the hit is still treated as a critical hit, and if they wear armor, a piece of their armor will "Rend" and they lose the AC benefit from that armor piece (see [Armorer’s Revision]()). If the target has a shield, they can choose to save their armor and have their shield break, instead.

**Lingering Injuries**  
A creature suffers one lingering injury if one or more of the following occurs:

1. They take damage equal to **half of their hit point maximum in one hit.**  
2. They are hit with an attack **while incapacitated.**  
3. They are hit with an attack that is successfully **“Crit Confirmed”** (above).

When a target suffers a lingering injury, the **type of injury is determined by rolling two d12s:**

1. The **first d12 roll** determines where the injury lands, with twelve bodily locations corresponding to armor piece placements. If the creature is wearing armor there, then that piece of armor is immediately broken.	  
   1. If the lingering injury was caused by a **Crit Confirm**, then the attack will not lead to a lingering injury if the piece of armor was not already rended or broken. If the armor is rended (or if there is no armor), then it breaks that piece of armor, and administers a lingering injury.  
2. The **second d12 roll** determines what type of lingering injury is delivered, outlined in the tables below. For instance, if Vengywen suffers an injury to his head, and then rolls a 6 on the second d12, then he will suffer a D: Guttering Wound. Ouch\!

Additionally, **some lingering injuries have frightful implications** for the wounded. Injuries marked with **(\!)** beside them give the wounded the [Staggered Condition]() each round until they receive enough healing to reach their hit point maximum.

Finally, if a creature goes long enough without healing, they might go septic. If the wounded has **not reached their hit point maximum 8 hours after receiving a lingering injury**, then they must make a CON Saving Throw DC \= (the hit point maximum \- their current hit points). Upon a failure, they receive a **Festering Wound**. Upon a success, they must roll the same saving throw (adjusted for their present hit points) each hour, until they can reach their hit point maximum.

**Festering Wound**  
You cannot typically take a [Long Rest or Hiatus](). Your hit point maximum is reduced by (22 \- CON Score (min 1)) every 24 hours the wound persists. If your hit point maximum drops to 0, you die. Healing a Festering Wound requires serious medical attention, helping the wounded to take a successful hiatus.

# Revised Resting & Recover Rules

**Revised Resting Rules**

In Elros, the world is dangerous to those who are unprepared. The following rules apply to resting, which reward the careful, meticulous adventurer, and provide ways to protect oneself from the horrors of combat.

**Short Rests**  
Short Rests last thirty minutes, during which a creature can often complete a light actively granted by their Vocation, or hone their weapon, fix a [rended piece of armor](), or some other activity.  
To heal, they can roll a number of hit dice equal to **double their proficiency bonus**, though they cannot expend more than they have stored. For each hit dice rolled, the creatures add their **Recovery skill modifier** to calculate the hit points gained.

**Long Rests**  
Long Rests last eight hours, wherein a creature sleeps for at least six, performing light activity for the other two. At the end of a long rest, the creature recovers half of their hit dice, and subsequently rolls as many hit dice as they wish, adding their **Recovery skill modifier** to each dice roll.

**Hiatus**  
A Hiatus lasts **three days**, during which a creature sleeps for at least six hours at least three times. A creature attempting a Hiatus may choose to roleplay these days, in which case they treat their first two sleeps as normal Long Rests. If they complete the third Long Rest without performing particularly physically taxing activity, such as acting in combat for a total of three turns per day, then they succeed their Hiatus.

Upon completion, a creature who completes a Hiatus gains the following benefits.

*  They regain all expended hit dice, plus and additional amount of hit dice equal to their proficiency bonus  
* They regain all lost hit points  
* They gain half their hit point maximum in temporary hit points  
* They have advantage on saving throws to resist the effects of disease until their next long rest  
* They recover all levels of exhaustion

Additionally, a character who suffered a Lingering Injury might attempt to heal it over the course of the Hiatus. In such a case, they must be healed by some effect other than resting by an amount specified on the Recovery Table below, each day. Alternatively, one can roll a Recovery Long Test over two hours, or receive a Medicine Long Test, subtracting the total from the amount they must be healed for that day.  
Over the course of the Hiatus, to complete the healing, the healing character must make a CON save Long Test. If the saving throw is failed, the creature can extend their Hiatus by one day, healing as usual, making the new check at the end of each successive day rather than restarting their Hiatus.

If a Lingering Injury is healed in this way, it leaves a **Minor Scar** in its place.

# Stamina Dice

**Expert Techniques**  
Expert Techniques are special effects that can apply when triggered by a player’s actions. These special attacks require Technique Points, a value determined semi-randomly by a number of brief roles.

Expert Techniques must be unlocked, typically by expended MMP. Once these techniques are unlocked, a character can use Stamina Dice to perform them.

Expert Techniques are listed here.  
The unlocking scheme is found here.

**Stamina Dice**

* A creature has a **maximum** number of Stamina Dice equal to **their proficiency bonus**  
  * A creature **recovers one expended Stamina Dice each turn, unless they took damage** between the beginning and end of their previous turn  
* Stamina Dice represent a character’s general ability, and available stamina to pull off more advanced techniques in combat  
* Stamina Dice also represent an element of randomness, according to the shifting opportunities represented dynamically in combat  
  * At the **start of each turn** of combat, the creature rolls all of their Stamina Dice, setting aside any **odd numbers as *inactive***, and holding onto any **even numbers as *active***, until the beginning of their next turn  
  * Inactive Stamina Dice are not expended, only unusable this round

**Active Stamina Dice** represent a pool of resources which a character can expend to perform Expert Techniques.

* To perform an Expert Technique, a character must **declare their intention**, and **then roll all Active Stamina Dice**  
* Each Expert Technique will have a **Technique Point** cost to perform successfully  
* Once all Active Stamina Dice are rolled, the character may select any combination of totals to reach the Technique Point cost of the selected Expert Technique  
  * **Upon a success**, the character expends a number of Stamina Dice whose total equal or exceed the selected Expert Technique, and performs the Expert Technique  
  * **Upon a failure** to meet the Technique Point cost of the selected Expert Technique, the character gains the [Reeling Condition](), and does not complete the technique.

# Expert Techniques

**Expert Techniques**  
Here is a list of Expert Techniques, which are in the middle of a rebalance. Four New Techniques are listed first, followed by a number of classic techniques.

Each Expert Technique has three tags.

1. The Prerequisite tag notes which weapon type(s) is required to learn and perform the Expert Technique  
2. The Cost tag notes the amount of [Martial Mastery Points]() required to learn the Technique  
3. The TP (Technique Points) tag notes the number of technique points must be expended to perform the technique

**Shield Batter (Prerequisites: Shield) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 2\)**  
Use your shield to control your opponent’s movement. Using a bonus action, or an attack (during the attack or an opportunity attack), make an attack roll against a target. Upon a hit, no damage is dealt, but the opponent is pushed back up to a distance determined by your STR bonus.

If your STR bonus is 0 or lower, then the distance is 5 feet. Otherwise, the distance is equal it your ((STR bonus/2) *rounded up* \* 5).

**Pinning Attack (Prerequisites: Dagger) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 2\)**  
When making a melee attack against a prone target, you can make a Pinning Attack. If the attack hits, the attack deals no damage, but the target will remain both grappled (by the dagger, not the attacker) and prone until a creature removes the dagger pinning them. This can be done using a bonus action, and requires a STR check; the DC for this is equal to the STR/DEX score of the creature who made the pinning attack.

A creature pinned with a dagger or a particular metal type treats all incoming attacks as being with the metal of the dagger.

**Parry (Prerequisites: Shortsword, Longsword, Glaive, Scimitar, Rapier, or**  
**Greatsword) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 3\)**  
At the start of any attack (though before dice have been rolled), you may use your reaction to parry. Doing so grants a bonus to your AC equal to half your proficiency bonus until the start of their next turn or until you’re not holding the weapon.

**Puncture Attack (Prerequisites: Spear, Halberd, Javelin, Trident, or Pike that has been made into a Harpoon) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 3\)**  
When you hit with an attack using a harpoon weapon within 30 feet, reroll your attack roll against the target's AC. Upon a success, the target is punctured. So long as this is the case and you hold an attached rope, they cannot move further away than 30 feet from you. They can attempt to yank the rope out of your hand by making a contested STR check as an action. If anyone but the target pulls the rope out, the punctured target takes the damage dice of the puncturing weapon again (without any bonuses). If the target pulls it out themself, this damage is rolled twice.

**Push Attack (Prerequisites: Bludgeoning Weapon) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 3\)**  
If you hit a creature with a melee attack, you can move it 10 feet away to an  
unoccupied space, provided the target is no more than one size larger than you.

**Slash Attack (Prerequisites: Slashing Weapon) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 3\)**  
When you make a melee attack, you can target all creatures within a cone with a range equal to a weapon's reach. You make an attack roll as normal, and deal damage to all targets hit in the area as though they were the only target of a melee weapon attack.

**Slowing Attack (Prerequisites: Shortsword, Longsword, Glaive, Billhook, Scimitar, Rapier, and Greatsword) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 3\)**  
When you make an opportunity attack, you can make a Slowing Attack. If you hit,  
you reduce the target’s speed by 5 multiplied by your proficiency bonus.

**Snatch (Prerequisites: Whip) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 3\)**  
Instead of making an attack, snatch an item. You must have an empty hand, the item must be equal to or under 5 pounds in weight. Make an attack roll DC 16 to successfully grab the object and pull it into your off hand. If the object you wish to grab is held by another creature, they may choose to contest the snatcher’s check by an opposed STR or DEX saving throw.

**Tripping Attack (Prerequisites: Flail, Morning-star, or Whip) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 3\)**  
When you make an opportunity attack, you can make a Tripping Attack. The target must succeed on a DEX saving throw (DC 8 \+ the attacker’s proficiency bonus \+ the attacker’s STR/DEX modifier) or be knocked prone. Targets one or more size categories larger than the attacker have advantage on this saving throw.

**Evading Attack (Prerequisites: Billhook, Flail, Morning-Star, Whip) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 3\)**  
When you make an attack, you ignore any AC increase a target benefits from using a shield, or their weapons.

**Longer Attack (Prerequisites: Spear, Halberd, Javelin, Trident, or Pike) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 3\)**  
As a bonus action on your turn, you increase your melee range by 5 feet (up to a maximum of 15 feet) until the start of your next turn.

**Lunge Attack (Prerequisites: Piercing Weapon) (Cost: 1 MMP) (TP: 4\)**  
Make a melee weapon attack that targets all creatures within a line to a maximum distance equal to the reach of the weapon used \+5 feet; the line only extends as far as there are targets within this maximum distance. Expend movement equal to this distance to make this attack, and roll an attack as normal. This attack deals damage to all creatures hit (as in, the attack roll was equal to or greater than their AC) in the area as though they were the only target of a melee weapon attack.

After the attack is completed, move a distance equal to the length of the line \+5 feet in the direction the attack was made, without incurring any opportunity attacks from targets hit in this attack.

**Position Control (Prerequisites: Tower Shield) (Cost: 2 MMP) (TP: 3\)**  
Prevent one target from making opportunity attacks against you. For no action or reaction, one target that would have the chance to make an opportunity attack against you does not.

**Trap Blade (Prerequisites: Wooden Shield) (Cost: 2 MMP) (TP: 4\)**  
When an attack roll using a piercing or slashing weapon misses you (that would have hit you if you had not equipped your wooden shield), you can use a reaction to make a STR check (with proficiency bonus if you are proficient with shields) against the attack roll total. Upon a success, you twist the attacking weapon out of the hand of the attacker, and toss it 10 feet in any direction.

If your shield is made of pine, your STR check is made with advantage.

**Shield Breaker (Prerequisites: Bludgeoning Weapon) (Cost: 2 MMP) (TP: 4\)**  
Attack an opponent's shield directly. Make an attack roll and use this technique to put a shield out of commission. If the attack role meets the target's AC, the shield is damaged. If the shield is made of wood, it no longer grants any AC bonus, until it is replaced or repaired.  If the shield is made out of metal, the bonus it applies to its user’s AC is reduced by 1\.

**Whittle Away (Prerequisites: Any Martial Weapon) (Cost: 2 MMP) (TP: 4\)**  
When you hit a target that is wearing armor that is damaged (broken or rended), you may expend technique points to forgo dealing damage, and instead rend a piece of armor adjacent to an already damaged piece.

**The Best Defense (Prerequisites: Any Martial Weapon) (Cost: 2 MMP) (TP: 4\)**  
If you are “just missed” by an attack roll, you can use your reaction to Stagger them. They gain the [Staggered Condition](https://docs.google.com/document/d/16sxUMOKtHs1oU01d8nGUVRgUqgPQITNe5ZcsyhQJaCY?tab=t.cmyw6324hou2).

**Spin Attack (Prerequisites: Slashing Weapon) (Cost: 2 MMP) (TP: 4\)**  
When you have taken the dodge action, you may choose to use your reaction and make a Spin Attack. Make an attack roll as normal, targeting all creatures within your melee range. This attack deals damage to all creatures hit, plus an additional roll of extra dice of damage equal to the highest physical damage dice used by the weapon. Once you have made the Spin Attack, you no longer benefit from the effects of a dodge.

**Knock ‘em Reeling (Prerequisites: Any Martial Weapon) (Cost: 2 MMP) (TP: 5\)**  
You may choose to attempt to give a target the [Reeling Condition](). Make your last attack roll of your attack action. If you hit, the attack does half damage, but the target is Reeling, until the end of your next turn.

**Arresting Attack (Prerequisites: Piercing Weapon) (Cost: 2 MMP) (TP: 5\)**  
When you have taken the dodge action, you may choose to use your reaction and make an Arresting Attack at any point until your next turn begins. If a target is hit with an Arresting Attack, you may choose one of the two following effects: the target’s speed is reduced to 0 until the start of their next turn OR the target is unable to take the attack action until the start of their next turn. Targets hit with an arresting attack take no damage. Once you have made the Arresting Attack, you no longer benefit from the effects of a dodge.

**Defensive Attack (Prerequisites: Billhook, Spear, Halberd, Javelin, Trident, or Pike) (Cost: 2 MMP) (TP: 5\)**  
When you have taken the dodge action, you may choose to use your reaction and make a Defensive Attack. Make a melee attack against it as a reaction, rolling with advantage. If the attack hits, the target takes an extra dice of damage equal to the highest physical damage dice used by the weapon. Once you have made the Defensive Attack, you no longer benefit from the effects of a dodge.

**Toppling Attack (Prerequisites: Warhammer, Warpick, Maul, Battleaxe, Greataxe, or Greatsword) (Cost: 2 MMP) (TP: 5\)**  
When you hit with a melee weapon attack, force the target to succeed on a STR saving throw (DC 8 \+ the attacker’s proficiency bonus \+ attacker's STR modifier) or be knocked prone. Targets one size category larger than the attacker have advantage on this saving throw, and targets two size categories larger automatically succeed.

**Opening Attack (Prerequisites: Bludgeoning Weapon that is also Heavy) (Cost: 2 MMP) (TP:5)**  
When you have taken the dodge action, you may choose to use your reaction and make an Opening Attack. If a target is hit with an Opening Attack, they take no damage. Instead, roll the number of Stamina Dice expended to use this Expert Technique, reducing the target’s AC by the total rolled until the end of their next turn. Once you have made the Opening Attack, you no longer benefit from the effects of a dodge.

**Struggle (Prerequisites: Any Martial Weapon) (Cost: 3 MMP) (TP: Special)**  
When you “just miss” (miss by one) on a melee attack roll against a creature, you can enter a struggle with that creature. If so, then you elect to add the total of active stamina dice to your attack roll. Once you declare the number of active stamina dice you will add to your roll, you must roll them immediately.

Once your new total is determined, the defender can roll any number of active stamina dice, subtracting the sum from your new total, until they 1\) surpass your new total, 2\) run out of dice, or 3\) elect to no longer contest your new total.

If your new total remains at or above the target’s AC, then the attack hits. If it does not, then your attack misses, and you are [Staggered]() until the start of your next turn.

**Superior Strike (Prerequisites: Any Weapon) (Cost: 3 MMP) (TP: 7\)**  
When you make an attack roll, choose to roll with advantage.

# Unlocking Techniques

**Martial Mastery Points**

In the interest of making martial combat more effective and dynamic, the following features and traits have been made available for all characters to unlock. Through training and significant feats in combat, characters now can upgrade their  
martial prowess.

**Martial Mastery Points (MMP)** are experience points that can be expended to  
increase skill with martial weapons. These are acquired when:

* A creature rolls a **Natural 20** on an attack roll against an enemy using a melee martial weapon  
* A creature rolls a **Natural 1** Athletics Check for training with martial

Weapons (This is a Long Check (pg. 51\) over 1 hour, which can be made once per  
long rest).

* These checks are using the *progressive endeavor* system. Roll a d20 on your first check, and if it fails, roll a d12 instead. If it fails again, roll a d10, etc, until you roll a d4. If you fail once more, then you automatically succeed. On a success, you are reset to a d20 on your next roll, and gain an MMP.

**Martial Mastery Points** may be expended by players during training. The following boons are available in this way for the listed  
amount of MMP:

* Gaining proficiency with a weapon type (such as glaives, or daggers)	  
  * Cost: 1 MMP  
* Learning an Expert Technique  
  * Cost: Listed with each Technique  
* Learning an Esoteric Technique  
  * Cost: Listed with each Technique  
  * Prerequisite: Must be training with someone who knows that technique  
* Training a Martial Stance  
  * Cost: 1 MMP per Rank attained  
* Gaining one of the eligible Feats  
  * Cost: 5 MMP

# Martial Stances

**Martial Stances**  
Martial Stances are features that allow access to additional abilities and perks, learned by spending MMP. Use a bonus action to enter a Martial Stance for one minute, or until you are knocked prone, petrified, incapacitated, or their movement speed is otherwise reduced to 0\. A creature can enter only one stance at a time, and can enter a stance a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus.

The physical requirements of entering a stance often leaves a warrior susceptible to certain types of attacks. In such a case, attacks from weapons (be they martial, natural, melee or ranged) that deal that damage type as their primary effect **are made with advantage** against a target who is in that stance.

**Precision Stance (*Slashing Susceptibility*)**  
**Rank 1**

* Once per turn, you may make a Precision Strike with a melee weapon, dealing an additional 1d6 \+ your proficiency bonus damage. Declare that you are making a Precision Strike before you roll to hit.  
* Targets hit with your Precision Strike suffer the Bleeding Condition, suffering 1d4 hit points of damage at the start of their turn, until they meet one of the following conditions:  
  * They are healed.  
  * They or an ally within 5 feet makes a Medicine Check DC 15 as an action to stop the bleeding.  
  * At the end of their turn, they make a CON saving throw DC your STR or DEX mod \+ your prof. Bonus \+ 6\.

**Rank 2**

* When rolling your Technique Dice, you may choose to reroll one of the dice.  
* The damage suffered due to the Bleeding Condition administered by your Precision Strike increases to 2d4.

**Rank 3**

* Your Precision Strike damage bonus increases to 2d6 \+ (your proficiency bonus) damage.  
* When a target is hit with your Precision Strike, you can expend an active stamina die to rend a piece of armor of your choosing.  
* Damage Susceptibility removed.

**Steady Stance (*Piercing Susceptibility*)**  
**Rank 1**

* When you miss with a melee weapon attack, you can expend an active Stamina Dice, rolling that dice and adding to total to your roll.  
* You gain a \+2 bonus to your concentration checks, and any saving throw that would knock you out of your stance.

**Rank 2**

* When rolling your Technique Dice, you may choose to reroll one of the dice once per turn.  
* Your Critical Hit Range with melee weapon attacks increases by 1\.

**Rank 3**

* When attempting to use the Superior Strike Expert Technique, you may roll an inactive stamina dice and add it to your TP total.  
* When you reroll a missed attack as a reaction (as per the Rank 1 ability of this Stance), you gain an additional 1d12 damage upon a hit.  
* Damage Susceptibility removed.

**Strider Stance (*Piercing Susceptibility*)**  
**Rank 1**

* Your movement speed increases by 5 feet.  
* Once per turn you may use your movement to Roll, which propels you 5 feet without incurring attacks of opportunity.

**Rank 2**

* Your movement speed increases by an additional 5 feet.  
* When in the Strider Stance, you gain an additional stamina dice.

**Rank 3**

* When in the Strider Stance, you gain an additional bonus action.  
* Damage Susceptibility removed.

**Duelist Stance (*Bludgeoning Susceptibility*)**  
**Rank 1**

* Whenever an enemy within the range of your wielded melee weapon makes a melee attack, you may spend your reaction to make a melee weapon attack against that creature first.

**Rank 2**

* Once per round, a target you hit with a melee weapon suffers the Bleeding condition 1d6.  
* When in the Duelist Stance, you gain an additional stamina dice.

**Rank 3**

* When in the Duelist Stance, you gain an additional reaction.  
* When you spend your reaction to make a melee weapon attack against an enemy, that attack is made with advantage.  
* Damage Susceptibility removed.

**Dual Wielder Stance (*Bludgeoning Susceptibility*)**  
**Rank 1**

* When you use your off-hand to make an attack, you may add your skill bonus to the off hand weapon attack.  
* When Dual Wielding, only your off hand weapon must have the light property, provided your main weapon does not have the heavy property.

**Rank 2**

* Once per turn, you may make an off-handed weapon attack without expending a bonus action.

**Rank 3**

* Whenever you make a weapon attack, you can always make an off handed weapon attack, without expending a bonus action.  
* Damage Susceptibility removed.

**Obstinance Stance**  
**Rank 1**

* Your speed is reduced by 10 feet, but you gain a \+1 bonus to your AC and physical saving throws.

**Rank 2**

* When you are hit with a melee weapon attack, you can make an opportunity attack as a reaction, dealing an additional 1d12 damage upon a hit.

**Rank 3**

* You gain an additional \+1 bonus to your AC and physical saving throws.

**Arcane Resistance Stance (*Melee Weapon Damage Susceptibility*)**  
**Rank 1**

* While in this stance, you gain a bonus to saving throws against spell effects equal to your proficiency bonus.

**Rank 2**

* When you hit a spellcaster with a melee weapon attack, they have disadvantage on any resulting concentration checks.

**Rank 3**

* The bonus to your saving throws against spell effects becomes equal to double your proficiency bonus.

# Conditions

**Condition: *Reeling***  
A reeling creature can take no actions or reactions (other than the reaction used to end the condition on itself). Additionally, once a creature has rolled an attack roll against a reeling target (but before the result of the roll has been announced), they can elect to expend one active stamina dice by rolling it, and either adding or subtracting the total to their initial attack roll. Reeling creatures hit with attack rolls which have been subtracted from in this way, or hit with critical hits, will become ***Staggered*** after the attack has been resolved.

A reeling creature can end the condition on themselves by using a reaction to expend an active stamina dice.

**Condition: *Staggered***  
When a creature is staggered, enemies can exploit their weaknesses for massive damage. When a melee attack hits a staggered target, the attacker may expend an active stamina dice to reroll their attack roll, as though rolling a critical confirm. If the reroll hits, their attack is treated as a critical hit. Additionally, staggered creatures have their speed set to 0\.

If a staggered creature takes the dodge action, they can choose to expend an active stamina dice to end the staggered condition, instead of the usual benefit of dodging. When a stagger is ended in this way, it also clears the creature of the **Reeling** condition.

**Condition: *Bleeding***  
A bleeding creature takes damage at the top of each of their turns equal to an amount specified by the source of this condition. Creatures are not vulnerable, resistant, or immune to this damage unless they have a feature which specifies that they are vulnerable, resistant, or immune or bleeding damage.

**Condition: *Dodging***  
Any creature who is missed by an attack while dodging regains one expended stamina die.

Additionally, here is the official 5e SRD description of the dodge action:

**Dodge**  
When you take the Dodge action, you focus entirely on avoiding attacks. Until the start of your next turn, any attack roll made against you has disadvantage if you can see the attacker, and you make Dexterity saving throws with advantage. You lose this benefit if you are incapacitated (as explained in Conditions) or if your speed drops to 0\.
