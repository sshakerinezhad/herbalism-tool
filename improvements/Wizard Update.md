# Wizard Update

**Mana & Discipline**  
*In Iridia, mana is a vital energy that actualizes the creative spirit of a creature. It is the ability to turn thoughts into magic. These thoughts are fixed by symbols, which specify the effects that are made real by this energy. These symbols might be divine seals, ancient runes, or arcane sigils which channel the palette of desires into the form of a particular spell.*

*Within all creatures subsists the endogenous spark of mana, which swells with the flashes of creativity cultivated by a creature. This increase in raw power is however distinct from the discipline required to fix this power into the forms of spells. Thus, as a Wizard advances they will both cultivate and discipline mana.*

A Wizard’s spell slots are determined by their class level, and their base mana is an amount equal to what is required to expend all of their spell slots. Below is a description of how a wizard can increase their mana.

**Mana & Knowledge Points**  
The primary way a Wizard acquires mana and knowledge points is by disciplining spell slots. At the end of a rest a Wizard can select any number of spells and discipline them, restricting the spells it can be used for to a particular school of magic *or* archemantic essence.

A Wizard can only discipline a spell slot in this way if they have the requisite material. For archemantic essences, it is a vital mineral of that essence. For schools of magic, it is a sigint ring with the sigil of that school of magic embossed upon the side, made of the right material:

**Transmutation — Calcified Mercury**  
**Graviturgy — Stone**  
**Conjuration — Gold**  
**Evocation — Brass**  
**Illusion — Silver**  
**Necromancy — Lead**  
**Chronomancy — Mithral**  
**Divination — Platinum**  
**Abjuration — Iron**  
**Enchantment — Fool’s Gold**

When a Wizard disciplines a spell slot, they attempt to roll a 1 on a die. On the first spell slot they discipline, they roll a d20, and if they do not roll a one, then a d12, then d10, d8, d6, d4, before treating the roll as an automatic success. Once the Wizard succeeds, the next roll starts again at a d20.

When a Wizard rolls a 1 whilst disciplining a spell slot, they earn **knowledge points** in that school or essence, and **mana**, both in an amount **equal to their proficiency bonus**.

**Wizard’s School of Magic**  
If a Wizard disciplines a spell slot for the school of magic that matches their subclass, they must roll a 1-3. Additionally, when a Wizard gains double knowledge points when earned for the school of magic that matches their subclass.

**Knowledge Points**  
Knowledge points represent a Wizards understanding of a particular school of magic or archemantic essence, and can be expended over a long rest to learn spells or arcane perks tied to that school or essence. Keep track of your knowledge points on the [progression table]().

**Learning spells** requires a Wizard to spend a number of knowledge points equal to the level of the spell in *both* the school of magic and the archmantic essence of the spell.

For example, to learn fireball, a third level evocation and fire essence spell, they must expend 3 knowledge points from both their evocation and fire pool.

**Arane Perks** are metamagical twists on existing spell effects. One knowledge point can be expended into a school or essence, to unlock the use of an arcane perk when casting spells of that school or essence. 

**Another Method of Gaining Knowledge Points**  
When a Wizard finds a spell scroll blueprint, they can choose to copy it into their book, or harvest a number of knowledge points equal to the spell’s level, distributed between the spell’s essence and school.

# Using Mana

**Upcasting**  
The minimum spell slot required to cast a spell is determined by the base level of the spell, and mana determines the power of the spell. And so, a mage can expend a leveled spell slot, but pour extra mana into the spell to achieve the effects of upcasting the spell.

Witchbolt is a first level spell, but by spending the amount of mana required to cast a second level spell, Witchbolt can be cast at second level, whilst still requiring only a first level spell slot.

**Arcana Perks**  
A skilled spellcaster is able to use mana to alter the effects of cast spells. The following features are available to unlock using knowledge points, and can be used by expending mana equal to the spell effect level times the metamagic modifier (MM) associated with the metamagic option (cantrips are spell level 0).

**Careful Spell (MM 2\)**  
When you cast a spell that forces other creatures to make a saving throw, you can protect some of those creatures from the spell's full force. Choose a number of those creatures up to your spellcasting modifier (minimum of one creature). A chosen creature automatically succeeds on its saving throw against the spell, and takes no damage from the spell.

**Distant Spell (MM 2\)**  
When you cast a spell that has a range of 5 feet or greater, you can double the range of the spell.  
When you cast a spell that has a range of touch, you can make the range of the spell 30 feet.

**Extended Spell (MM 3\)**  
When you cast a spell that has a duration of 1 minute or longer, you can double its duration, to a maximum duration of 24 hours.

**Heightened Spell** **(MM 3\)**  
When you cast a spell that forces a creature to make a saving throw to resist its effects, you can give one target of the spell disadvantage on its first saving throw made against the spell.

**Quickened Spell (MM 3\)**  
When you cast a spell that has a casting time of 1 action, you can change the casting time to 1 bonus action for this casting.

**Seeking Spell (MM 2\)**  
If you make an attack roll for a spell and miss, you can reroll the d20, and you must use the new roll.

**Subtle Spell (MM 3\)**  
When you cast a spell, you can cast it without any somatic or verbal components.

**Transmuted Spell (MM 1.5)**  
When you cast a spell that deals a type of damage from the following list, you can spend 1 sorcery point to change that damage type to one of the other listed types: acid, cold, fire, lightning, poison, thunder.

**Twinned Spell (MM 2\)**  
When you cast a spell that targets only one creature and doesn't have a range of self, you can target a second creature in range with the same spell (costs 2 Mana if the spell is a cantrip). To be eligible, a spell must be incapable of targeting more than one creature at the spell's current level. For example, magic missile and scorching ray aren't eligible, but ray of frost and chromatic orb are.

**Overchannel (MM 3 \- Special)**  
When you cast a spell you may deal maximum damage on the first damage roll of the spell. Additionally, the strain of over-channeling your innate magic causes you to take 1d6 necrotic damage for each spell effect level of the casting. This damage cannot be reduced or avoided in any way.
